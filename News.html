SpaceX will fly space tourists to the ISS as soon as next year

The 10-day mission will use the company's Crew Dragon capsule

Houston-based private space company Axiom has forged a patnership with SpaceX to fly paying tourists to the ISS as soon as the second half of 20201. Under the terms of their deal, SpaceX will use the Crew Dragon capsule to ferry three tourists and the Axiom staff member, who will make sure they don't interfer with NASA crew members doing their job, to the orbiting lab. It's worth nothing that Axiom was the company that NASA chose to build the space station's first commercial destinantion module.
